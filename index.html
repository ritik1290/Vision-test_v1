Lens Disorders

Cataracts

Clouding of the eye's natural lens, leading to blurred vision and sensitivity to glare.

Learn More

Presbyopia

Age-related loss of ability to focus on close objects, typically beginning after age 40.

Learn More

Retinal Disorders

Macular Degeneration (AMD)

Leading cause of vision loss in older adults, affecting central vision needed for reading and driving.

Learn More

Diabetic Retinopathy

A diabetes complication that damages blood vessels in the retina, potentially causing blindness.

Learn More

Retinal Detachment

Medical emergency where the retina pulls away from its normal position, requiring immediate treatment.

Learn More

Retinitis Pigmentosa

Inherited degenerative disease causing progressive loss of night and peripheral vision.

Learn More

Central Serous Retinopathy

Fluid accumulation under the retina causing temporary vision loss, often affecting young adults.

Learn More

Macular Hole

A small defect in the center of the macula causing blurred and distorted central vision.

Learn More

Epiretinal Membrane

Scar tissue on the retina's surface causing vision distortion and blurriness (macular pucker).

Learn More

Retinal Vein Occlusion

Blockage of veins carrying blood away from the retina, causing sudden vision loss.

Learn More

Retinal Artery Occlusion

Blockage of the main artery to the retina, a medical emergency causing sudden vision loss.

Learn More

Vitreous Hemorrhage

Bleeding into the gel-filled center of the eye, causing floaters and vision obstruction.

Learn More

Posterior Vitreous Detachment

Age-related separation of vitreous gel from retina causing floaters and flashes.

Learn More

Choroidal Neovascularization

Abnormal blood vessels growing from choroid into retina, most common in wet AMD.

Learn More

Optic Nerve Disorders

Glaucoma

Group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, often due to high eye pressure.

Learn More

Angle-Closure Glaucoma

Medical emergency with sudden eye pressure spike, severe pain, and blurred vision.

Learn More

Optic Neuritis

Inflammation of the optic nerve causing sudden vision loss and pain with eye movement.

Learn More

Ocular Hypertension

Elevated eye pressure without glaucoma damage, requires monitoring and possible treatment.

Learn More

Corneal Disorders

Keratoconus

Progressive thinning and bulging of the cornea into a cone shape, distorting vision.

Learn More

Corneal Ulcer

Open sore on the cornea, usually from infection, requiring urgent treatment.

Learn More

Fuchs' Dystrophy

Gradual deterioration of corneal cells causing swelling, glare, and cloudy vision.

Learn More

Corneal Abrasion

Scratch or scrape on the cornea's surface causing pain and light sensitivity.

Learn More

Pterygium

Growth of pink, fleshy tissue on the conjunctiva, often from UV exposure.

Learn More

Pinguecula

Yellowish, slightly raised deposit on the conjunctiva, usually harmless.

Learn More

Keratitis

Inflammation or infection of the cornea causing redness, pain, and blurred vision.

Learn More

Band Keratopathy

Calcium deposition in cornea from chronic inflammation or elevated calcium levels.

Learn More

Refractive Errors

Myopia (Nearsightedness)

Difficulty seeing distant objects clearly while near objects appear sharp.

Learn More

Hyperopia (Farsightedness)

Difficulty focusing on close objects; distant objects may be clearer.

Learn More

Astigmatism

Irregular curvature of the cornea or lens causing blurred or distorted vision.

Learn More

Inflammatory & Infectious Disorders

Conjunctivitis (Pink Eye)

Inflammation or infection of the conjunctiva causing redness, discharge, and irritation.

Learn More

Uveitis

Inflammation of the uvea (middle layer of the eye) causing redness, pain, and vision changes.

Learn More

Iritis (Anterior Uveitis)

Inflammation of the iris causing eye pain, light sensitivity, and blurred vision.

Learn More

Scleritis

Severe inflammation of the sclera (white of the eye) causing intense pain and redness.

Learn More

Episcleritis

Benign inflammation of episclera causing mild redness and irritation.

Learn More

Eyelid & Surface Disorders

Dry Eye Syndrome

Insufficient tear production or poor tear quality causing discomfort and vision fluctuation.

Learn More

Blepharitis

Chronic eyelid inflammation causing redness, crusting and irritation along the eyelid margins.

Learn More

Chalazion

Painless bump on the eyelid from a blocked oil gland, different from an infection.

Learn More

Stye (Hordeolum)

Painful, red bump on the eyelid edge caused by bacterial infection of an eyelash follicle.

Learn More

Ectropion

Outward turning of the eyelid, exposing the inner surface and causing irritation.

Learn More

Entropion

Inward turning of the eyelid causing lashes to rub against the eye.

Learn More

Dacryocystitis

Infection of the lacrimal sac causing painful swelling near the nose.

Learn More

Nasolacrimal Duct Obstruction

Blocked tear drainage system causing excessive tearing and discharge.

Learn More

Strabismus

Learn More

Retinoblastoma

Most common eye cancer in children, characterized by white pupil reflex (leukocoria).

Ocular Melanoma

Most common primary eye cancer in adults, arising from pigmented cells in uveal tract.

Learn More
